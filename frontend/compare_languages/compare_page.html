What Makes a Language "Easier to Learn"?

The more similar languages are, i.e the less "new" stuff you have to know,
the easier they are to learn. With this in mind, lɪŋˈ-guage has developed a system
that stores information on languages like where they're from, what their grammar
and writing looks like - and even how many sounds they have. Then lɪŋˈ-guage pushes
all of this into an equation that compares these traits, and gets out a number that
represents how different they are from eachother.

This number is what lɪŋˈ-guage uses to sort the language comparisons, presenting
them to you in order of most similair, and easiest to learn, to most the different,and
hardest to learn.
